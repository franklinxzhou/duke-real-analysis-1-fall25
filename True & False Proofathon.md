## 1–30: Metric spaces, compactness, connectedness

1. **T/F** In _every_ metric space, every closed and bounded subset is compact.
2. **T/F** In $\mathbb{R}^k$ with the usual Euclidean metric, every closed and bounded subset is compact.
3. **T/F** Every compact subset of a metric space is closed and bounded.
4. **T/F** Every closed subset of a compact metric space is compact.
5. **T/F** Every compact subset of $\mathbb{R}$ is a finite union of closed intervals.
6. **T/F** Every infinite compact subset of $\mathbb{R}$ has at least one limit point in R\mathbb{R}R.
7. **T/F** In any metric space, the continuous image of a compact set is compact.
8. **T/F** In any metric space, the inverse image of a compact set under a continuous function is compact.
9. **T/F** In a metric space, a set is compact if and only if it is complete and totally bounded.
10. **T/F** Every complete subset of a metric space is closed in that metric space.
11. **T/F** Every closed subset of a complete metric space is complete (with the induced metric).
12. **T/F** Every complete subset of $\mathbb{R}$ (with the usual metric) is closed in $\mathbb{R}$.
13. **T/F** Every closed subset of $\mathbb{R}^k$ (with the usual metric) is complete.
14. **T/F** Every bounded sequence in a metric space has a convergent subsequence.
15. **T/F** Every sequence in a compact metric space has a convergent subsequence.
16. **T/F** In a metric space, every sequence has a Cauchy subsequence.
17. **T/F** In $\mathbb{R}^k$, every bounded sequence has a convergent subsequence.
18. **T/F** Any continuous bijection from a compact metric space onto a metric space is a homeomorphism.
19. **T/F** Any continuous bijection from a compact subset of $\mathbb{R}$ (with subspace metric) onto a subset of $\mathbb{R}$ has a continuous inverse.
20. **T/F** There exists a continuous bijection from $[0,1]$ onto $(0,1)$.
21. **T/F** If $(X,d)$ is a metric space and $E\subset X$ is compact, then $E$ is complete (with the induced metric).
22. **T/F** If $E\subset \mathbb{R}$ is connected, then $E$ is an interval (possibly a single point).
23. **T/F** The continuous image of a connected set is connected.
24. **T/F** The inverse image of a connected set under a continuous function is connected.
25. **T/F** If $E\subset \mathbb{R}$ is compact and connected, then $E=[a,b]$ for some $a\le b$.
26. **T/F** Every continuous function from a compact, connected subset of $\mathbb{R}$ into $\mathbb{R}$ takes every value between its minimum and maximum.
27. **T/F** A continuous function from a compact metric space into R\mathbb{R}R which has no zeros must satisfy either $f(x)>0$ for all $x$ or $f(x)<0$ for all $x$.
28. **T/F** If a metric space $X$ is compact, then every infinite subset of $X$ has a limit point in $X$.
29. **T/F** In $\mathbb{R}$, if a set $E$ has the property that every infinite subset of $E$ has a limit point in $E$, then $E$ is compact.
30. **T/F** On $\mathbb{R}$ with metric $d'(x,y)=\min\{1,|x-y|\}$, a subset is compact if and only if it is closed and bounded in the usual sense.

## 31–50: Sequences and series of real numbers

31. **T/F** Every Cauchy sequence in a metric space is bounded.
32. **T/F** Every bounded sequence in a metric space has a Cauchy subsequence.
33. **T/F** Let $(x_n)$ be a sequence in a metric space. If there exists $x$ such that _every_ subsequence of $(x_n)$ has a further subsequence converging to $x$, then $x_n\to x$.
34. **T/F** If $\sum a_n$ converges, then $a_n\to 0$.
35. **T/F** There exists a series $\sum a_n$​ with $a_n\to 0$ such that $\sum a_n$​ diverges.
36. **T/F** If $\sum a_n$ converges absolutely, then it converges conditionally.
37. **T/F** If $\sum a_n$​ converges conditionally, then its sequence of partial sums is bounded.
38. **T/F** Let $(a_n)$ be a decreasing sequence of positive numbers with $\lim_{n\to\infty} n a_n = 0$. Then $\sum a_n$ converges.
39. **T/F** If $(a_n)$ is a sequence of nonnegative numbers and $\sum a_n$​ converges, then $\sum \sqrt{a_n}$ converges.
40. **T/F** Let $(a_n)$ be a decreasing sequence of positive numbers. If $\sum a_n$ diverges, then $\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n\ne 0$.
41. **T/F** Every rearrangement of an absolutely convergent series converges to the same sum.
42. **T/F** Every rearrangement of a conditionally convergent series converges, but possibly to a different sum.
43. **T/F** If $\sum a_n​$ and $\sum b_n$​ are convergent series of real numbers, then $\sum a_n b_n$​ converges.
44. **T/F** If $\sum a_n​$ converges and $\sum b_n$​ converges absolutely, then $\sum a_n b_n$ converges absolutely.
45. **T/F** If a real sequence has at most one limit point, then it converges.
46. **T/F** If a bounded real sequence has a unique limit point $L$, then it converges to $L$.
47. **T/F** If a real sequence has a convergent subsequence, then the sequence is bounded.
48. **T/F** If a real series $\sum a_n$​ converges absolutely, then the series $\sum a_n^2$ also converges absolutely.
49. **T/F** There exists a real series $\sum a_n$ that converges but for which $\sum a_n^2$ diverges.
50. **T/F** If $\sum a_n$​ is a series of nonnegative terms that diverges, then its sequence of partial sums is unbounded.

## 51–70: Continuity and uniform continuity

51. **T/F** Every uniformly continuous function $f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}$ can be extended to a continuous function on $[0,1]$.
52. **T/F** Every continuous function $f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}$ can be extended to a continuous function on $[0,1]$.
53. **T/F** Every continuous function on a compact metric space is uniformly continuous.
54. **T/F** Every uniformly continuous function on a metric space is continuous.
55. **T/F** There exists a continuous function from a compact metric space to $\mathbb{R}$ that is unbounded.
56. **T/F** The continuous image of a connected set is connected.
57. **T/F** If $f:X\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and its range $f(X)$ is compact in $\mathbb{R}$, then $f$ is uniformly continuous on $X$.
58. **T/F** If $f:[0,\infty)\to\mathbb{R}$ is uniformly continuous and bounded, then $\lim_{x\to\infty} f(x)$ exists.
59. **T/F** If $f,g:X\to\mathbb{R}$ are continuous and $X$ is compact, then $f\cdot g$ is uniformly continuous on $X$.
60. **T/F** If $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and periodic, then $f$ is uniformly continuous on $\mathbb{R}$.
61. **T/F** If $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and one-to-one, then $f$ is strictly monotone.
62. **T/F** If $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and strictly monotone, then its inverse $f^{-1}$ is continuous on its domain.
63. **T/F** There exists a function $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ which is continuous at every irrational point and discontinuous at every rational point.
64. **T/F** If $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous everywhere and satisfies $f(\mathbb{Q})\subset\mathbb{Q}$, then $f$ is constant.
65. **T/F** Every continuous injective function $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is surjective.
66. **T/F** Every continuous function $f:[0,1]\to[0,1]$ has at least one fixed point, i.e., a point $x$ with $f(x)=x$.
67. **T/F** If $f:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and $f(0)\cdot f(1)>0$, then $f(x)\neq 0$ for all $x\in[0,1]$.
68. **T/F** If $f:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous, $f(x)\ge 0$ for all $x$, and $\int_0^1 f(x)\,dx=0$, then $f(x)=0$ for all $x\in[0,1]$.
69. **T/F** If $f:[0,1]\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous on $[0,1]$, differentiable on $(0,1)$, and $f'(x)=0$ for all $x\in(0,1)$, then $f$ is constant on $[0,1]$.
70. **T/F** If $f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}$ is continuous and satisfies $f(x+y)=f(x)+f(y)$ for all $x,y\in\mathbb{R}$, then there is a constant $c$ such that $f(x)=cx$ for all $x$.

## 71–80: Differentiation

71. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable at $a$, then $f$ is continuous at $a$.
72. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $\mathbb{R}$ and $f'$ is bounded, then $f$ is uniformly continuous on $\mathbb{R}$.
73. **T/F** There exists a differentiable function $f:(0,1)\to\mathbb{R}$ that is not uniformly continuous.
74. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on an open interval $(a,b)$ and $f'(x)$ is continuous on $(a,b)$, then $f$ is twice differentiable on $(a,b)$.
75. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$ and $f'$ is bounded on $(a,b)$, then $f$ is Lipschitz on $(a,b)$.
76. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$ and $f'(x)=0$ at infinitely many points of $(a,b)$, then $f$ is constant.
77. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$ and $f'$ is increasing on $(a,b)$, then $f$ is convex on $(a,b)$.
78. **T/F** If $f$ is convex on $(a,b)$, then $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$.
79. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$, $f′$ is continuous, and $f'(x)\neq 0$ for all $x\in(a,b)$, then $f$ is either strictly increasing or strictly decreasing on $(a,b)$.
80. **T/F** If $f$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$ and $f'(x)\ge 0$ for all $x\in(a,b)$, then $f$ is increasing (nondecreasing) on $(a,b)$.

## 81–90: (Riemann) integration

81. **T/F** Every continuous function on $[a,b]$ is Riemann integrable.
82. **T/F** Every monotone function on $[a,b]$ is Riemann integrable.
83. **T/F** There exists a bounded function on $[0,1]$ which is not Riemann integrable.
84. **T/F** Every bounded function on $[a,b]$ that is continuous except at finitely many points is Riemann integrable.
85. **T/F** Every bounded function on $[a,b]$ that is continuous except on a countable set is Riemann integrable.
86. **T/F** If $f$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$, then $|f|$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$.
87. **T/F** If $|f|$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$, then $f$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$.
88. **T/F** If $f$ and $g$ are Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$, then $f\cdot g$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$.
89. **T/F** If $f\ge 0$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$ and $\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = 0$, then $f(x)=0$ for all $x\in[a,b]$.
90. **T/F** If $f$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$, then $F(x)=\displaystyle\int_a^x f(t)\,dt$ is differentiable for every $x\in(a,b)$.

## 91–100: Sequences & series of functions, equicontinuity

91. **T/F** If $(f_n)$ is a sequence of continuous functions on $[0,1]$ converging uniformly to $f$, then $f$ is continuous on $[0,1]$.
92. **T/F** There exists a sequence of continuous functions on $[0,1]$ that converges pointwise to a discontinuous function.
93. **T/F** There exists a sequence of discontinuous functions on $[0,1]$ that converges uniformly to a continuous function.
94. **T/F** Let $(f_n)$ be continuous functions on $[0,1]$ that converge uniformly to $f$. If $f_n(x)\ge 0$ for all $x$ and all $n$, then $f(x)\ge 0$ for all $x$.
95. **T/F** If $(f_n)$ are Riemann integrable functions on $[a,b]$ that converge uniformly to $f$, then $f$ is Riemann integrable and $\displaystyle\int_a^b f = \lim_{n\to\infty} \int_a^b f_n$.
96. **T/F** Let $(f_n)$ be differentiable on $(a,b)$, suppose $f_n(x_0)$ converges for some $x_0\in(a,b)$, and suppose $(f_n')$ converges uniformly on $(a,b)$ to a function $g$. Then there is a differentiable function $f$ on $(a,b)$ such that $f_n\to f$ uniformly on every closed subinterval of $(a,b)$ and $f'=g$.
97. **T/F** Suppose $(f_n)$ are continuous on $[0,1]$ and converge uniformly to $f$. If each $f_n$​ is Lipschitz with the same Lipschitz constant $L$, then $f$ is Lipschitz with constant $L$.
98. **T/F** If $(f_n)$ is an equicontinuous and pointwise bounded family of real-valued functions on $[0,1]$, then there exists a subsequence $(f_{n_k})$ that converges uniformly on $[0,1]$.
99. **T/F** If $(f_n)$ is a sequence of continuous functions on $[0,1]$ that converges pointwise to $f$, and $(f_n)$ is equicontinuous, then $f$ is continuous on $[0,1]$.
100. **T/F** If $(f_n)$ is a sequence of continuous functions on $[0,1]$ such that for every $x\in[0,1]$ the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty f_n(x)$ converges, then the series $\sum_{n=1}^\infty f_n$​ converges uniformly on $[0,1]$.
